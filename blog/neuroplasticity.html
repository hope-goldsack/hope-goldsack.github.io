Neuroplasticity

How does understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefit people?

  • Visualising and imagining the practice has added value alongside physical practice to the connection pathways and strengthens them to keep strong pathways.
  • Intelligence is relative, it can change if your mind realises its ability of growth rather than stagnation.

How might you engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit?

  • The learning journey journal is a fantastic way to start, this will help me to visualise and imagine the work I have done so it is added value alongside my physical practice.
  • Reflecting will not only reinforce my learning but it will also help me realise my capability to improve my intelligence, become more confident and positive about my abilities so I can learn to believe I have the capacity to grow intelligence.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

  • Commit to engaging in the exercise as frequently as you can both in imagining and physical practice.
  • Have good control over my timetable as well as my rest time, use it well to increase my ability to focus.
  • Make errors, test myself, find solutions, become frustrated and come back to try again. While also considering my challenges and thinking about these practices and how they can be navigated.

Growth Mindset

What is Growth Mindset and why it is relevant?

Development and growth is important to the course, it isn’t just about getting everything right it is about the challenges that come with it, and the help/advice you receive. Growth mindset is changing the way you train your brain to think, rather than using a typical fixed mindset. The Harvard Business Review defines it as “people who believe their talents can be developed (through hard work, good strategies, and input from others) have a growth mindset. It is relevant here as there will be challenges, with a positive growth mindset a better belief in your ability will enhance learning.

Were there any suprises or changes during this exploration?

I have been studying for a long time & I tend to challenge myself with new challenges within my life very often. I have a strong belief that when using a positive attitude that I can learn and have the capability to develop further to increase my overall intelligence and learning journeys.

Intergrating a Growth Mindset into my learning journey

  • To embrace challenges as positive learning experiences to learn and develop a better understanding of the problems.
  • As quoted before, hard work will improve confidence, as well as increasing a positive mindset.
  • Good strategies, such as effective time management.

A lot of this blog was written when these concepts were first introduced at the start of the course. As the course has gone on I have found myself adapting my learning style. Knowing these concepts I have had so much more confidence in so many areas, especially trusting my gut, believing I have the capacity and intelligence to solve big issues myself - which has actually enabled me to be a better coder. I have tried to explain my learning to others, and spent more time mentally practicing the topics we have been introduced to which really has had a huge impact on my learning. I will continue to reflect on these concepts and utilise them as much as I can throughout this course and into the future. You really never stop learning!
